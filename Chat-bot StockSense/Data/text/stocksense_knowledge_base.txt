1. What is a stock?
A stock (also called a share) is a small piece of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a part-owner of that company. If the company does well and makes profit, the value of your stock goes up and you can sell it for more than you paid. Companies sell stocks to raise money for their business, and investors buy stocks hoping to earn returns over time.

2. What is the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX)?
The Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) is the only stock exchange in Pakistan where stocks of Pakistani companies are bought and sold. It was formed in 2016 by merging the Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad stock exchanges. PSX is located in Karachi and is regulated by the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP). Over 500 companies are listed on PSX, and the main index is the KSE-100 which tracks the top 100 companies.

3. What is the KSE-100 index?
The KSE-100 index is the benchmark index of the Pakistan Stock Exchange. It tracks the performance of the top 100 companies listed on PSX by market capitalization. When people say "the market went up" or "the market crashed", they are usually referring to the KSE-100 index. If the KSE-100 goes up, it means most of the top stocks gained value that day. If it goes down, most stocks lost value.

4. What is the KSE-30 index?
The KSE-30 index tracks the top 30 companies on the Pakistan Stock Exchange based on market capitalization and liquidity. These are the biggest and most actively traded companies in Pakistan. StockSense focuses on KSE-30 stocks for predictions because they have the most reliable data and highest trading volumes.

5. How do I start investing in stocks in Pakistan?
To start investing in stocks in Pakistan, follow these steps: (1) Choose a SECP-registered broker - you can find a list on the PSX website. (2) Open a trading account with the broker - you will need your CNIC, bank account details, and a recent photograph. (3) Complete KYC (Know Your Customer) verification. (4) Get a UIN (Unique Identification Number) from NCCPL. (5) Open a CDC sub-account for holding your shares electronically. (6) Deposit money into your trading account. (7) Start buying and selling stocks through your broker's trading platform or app.

6. How much money do I need to start investing?
You can start investing with as little as Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 10,000. Many stocks on PSX are priced under Rs. 50, so even a small amount lets you buy a few shares. There is no official minimum requirement - you just need enough to buy at least one share of the stock you want. It is recommended to start small, learn how the market works, and gradually increase your investment as you gain experience.

7. What is a broker?
A broker is a licensed company or person that helps you buy and sell stocks on the Pakistan Stock Exchange. You cannot buy stocks directly from PSX - you must go through a registered broker. Brokers charge a small commission (usually 0.1% to 0.5%) on each trade. You can find SECP-registered brokers on the PSX website. Some popular brokers in Pakistan include AKD Securities, Topline Securities, JS Global, and Arif Habib.

8. What is a dividend?
A dividend is a payment that a company makes to its shareholders from its profits. When a company earns profit, it can either reinvest the money back into the business or distribute some of it to shareholders as dividends. Dividends are usually paid annually or semi-annually. For example, if you own 100 shares of a company and it declares a Rs. 5 per share dividend, you will receive Rs. 500. Not all companies pay dividends - some prefer to reinvest all profits for growth.

9. What is an IPO?
IPO stands for Initial Public Offering. It is when a private company sells its shares to the public for the first time and gets listed on the stock exchange. Through an IPO, regular investors can buy shares of the company at the offering price. After the IPO, the shares start trading on PSX and the price is determined by supply and demand. IPOs can be a good investment opportunity but also carry risk since the company has no public track record yet.

10. What is a bull market and bear market?
A bull market is when stock prices are generally rising and investors are optimistic. The term comes from a bull that thrusts its horns upward. A bear market is when stock prices are falling and investors are pessimistic. The term comes from a bear that swipes its paws downward. In Pakistan, when the KSE-100 is consistently going up over weeks or months, we say it is a bull market. When it keeps falling, it is a bear market.

11. What is market capitalization?
Market capitalization (market cap) is the total value of a company's shares. It is calculated by multiplying the current stock price by the total number of shares. For example, if a company's stock price is Rs. 100 and it has 10 million shares, its market cap is Rs. 1 billion. Large-cap companies are big, established companies (like HBL, OGDC), while small-cap companies are smaller and more risky.

12. What is the difference between trading and investing?
Trading means buying and selling stocks frequently (daily, weekly) to profit from short-term price changes. Investing means buying stocks and holding them for months or years, expecting the company to grow in value over time. Trading is riskier and requires more time and attention. Investing is generally considered safer for beginners because it does not require constant monitoring. Most financial experts recommend long-term investing for regular people.

13. Is investing in stocks safe?
Investing in stocks carries risk - you can lose money if stock prices fall. However, historically, the stock market has provided better returns than bank savings accounts over long periods. The key risks are: market risk (prices fall due to economic conditions), company risk (the specific company performs poorly), and liquidity risk (you cannot sell quickly). To reduce risk, diversify your portfolio across multiple stocks and sectors, invest for the long term, and never invest money you cannot afford to lose.

14. What is a portfolio?
A portfolio is the collection of all stocks and investments you own. For example, if you own shares of OGDC, HBL, and LUCK, those three stocks together make up your portfolio. A well-diversified portfolio contains stocks from different sectors (banking, energy, cement, etc.) to reduce risk. If one sector performs poorly, stocks in other sectors may still do well, balancing out your losses.

15. What is SECP?
SECP stands for the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan. It is the government body that regulates the stock market, brokers, and listed companies in Pakistan. SECP's job is to protect investors, ensure fair trading, and maintain transparency in the capital market. If you have a complaint against a broker or company, you can file it with SECP.

16. What is CDC?
CDC stands for the Central Depository Company of Pakistan. It holds your shares electronically in a secure account (called a CDC sub-account). In the old days, shares were physical paper certificates that could be lost or stolen. Now all shares are held electronically by CDC. When you buy shares through your broker, they are credited to your CDC account. You can check your holdings online through the CDC Access portal.

17. What is NCCPL?
NCCPL stands for the National Clearing Company of Pakistan Limited. It handles the clearing and settlement of stock trades on PSX. When you buy a stock, NCCPL ensures that the shares are transferred from the seller to you, and the money is transferred from you to the seller. Settlement in Pakistan follows a T+2 cycle, meaning the trade is settled 2 business days after the transaction.

18. What are the trading hours of PSX?
The Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) trades from Monday to Friday. Trading hours are from 9:30 AM to 3:30 PM Pakistan Standard Time (PST). The market is closed on Saturdays, Sundays, and Pakistani public holidays. Pre-open session starts at 9:15 AM where orders are placed but not executed until the market opens at 9:30 AM.

19. What is a P/E ratio?
P/E ratio stands for Price-to-Earnings ratio. It tells you how much investors are willing to pay for each rupee of a company's profit. It is calculated by dividing the stock price by the earnings per share (EPS). For example, if a stock price is Rs. 100 and the EPS is Rs. 10, the P/E ratio is 10. A lower P/E may indicate the stock is undervalued (cheap), while a higher P/E may indicate it is overvalued (expensive). However, P/E should be compared within the same sector.

20. What sectors are available on PSX?
The Pakistan Stock Exchange has companies from many sectors including: Banking (HBL, MCB, UBL, Meezan Bank), Oil and Gas (OGDC, PPL, POL), Cement (Lucky Cement, DG Khan Cement, Cherat Cement), Fertilizer (Engro, FFC, EFERT), Power (Hub Power), Technology (Systems Limited), Pharmaceuticals (Searle Company), Refinery (Attock Refinery, Pakistan Refinery), and many more. Banking and energy are the largest sectors by market capitalization.

21. What is a stop-loss order?
A stop-loss order is an instruction to your broker to automatically sell a stock if its price drops to a certain level. It helps limit your losses. For example, if you buy a stock at Rs. 100 and set a stop-loss at Rs. 90, the stock will automatically be sold if the price falls to Rs. 90, limiting your loss to Rs. 10 per share. Stop-loss orders are an important risk management tool.

22. What are blue chip stocks?
Blue chip stocks are shares of large, well-established, and financially stable companies with a history of reliable performance. In Pakistan, blue chip stocks include companies like OGDC, HBL, MCB, Lucky Cement, and Engro. These companies are leaders in their sectors, pay regular dividends, and are considered less risky than smaller companies. Blue chip stocks are generally recommended for beginner investors.

23. Should I invest in stocks or keep money in bank?
Bank savings accounts in Pakistan typically offer 10-15% annual returns. Historically, the stock market has provided higher returns over long periods (15-25% average annual returns on KSE-100 over the past decade). However, stocks are riskier - you can lose money in bad years. A balanced approach is to keep an emergency fund in your bank account (3-6 months of expenses) and invest the rest in stocks for long-term growth. Never invest money you need for daily expenses.

24. What is short selling?
Short selling means selling a stock you do not own, hoping to buy it back later at a lower price. In Pakistan, short selling is allowed under regulated conditions on PSX. It is a very advanced and risky strategy - if the stock price goes up instead of down, you could lose a lot of money. Short selling is not recommended for beginners.

25. How do I know which stock to buy?
To choose which stock to buy, consider these factors: (1) Look at the company's financial health - is it profitable? Is revenue growing? (2) Check the P/E ratio compared to sector average. (3) Look at the company's dividend history. (4) Consider the sector outlook - is the industry growing? (5) Check our AI predictions on StockSense for data-driven recommendations. (6) Diversify - don't put all your money in one stock. (7) Start with blue chip stocks if you are a beginner.

32. What is RSI?
RSI stands for Relative Strength Index. It is a number between 0 and 100 that measures how fast a stock's price is rising or falling. RSI above 70 means the stock may be overbought (price went up too fast and might come back down). RSI below 30 means the stock may be oversold (price dropped too much and might bounce back). RSI between 30-70 is considered normal. Our AI model uses RSI as one of its input features.

33. What is SMA?
SMA stands for Simple Moving Average. It is the average closing price of a stock over a specific number of days. SMA-20 is the average over the last 20 trading days, and SMA-50 is the average over the last 50 days. When the stock price is above its SMA, it generally indicates an upward trend. When the price crosses below the SMA, it may signal a downward trend. StockSense uses both SMA-20 and SMA-50 in its predictions.

34. What is volume in stocks?
Volume is the number of shares traded in a day. High volume means many people are buying and selling the stock, which usually means higher interest and liquidity. Low volume means few people are trading, which can make it harder to buy or sell at your desired price. A sudden spike in volume often signals important news or a major price move. StockSense shows volume data for all KSE-30 stocks.

35. What is a trading account?
A trading account is an account you open with a SECP-registered stock broker that allows you to buy and sell shares on the Pakistan Stock Exchange. To open one, you need: a valid CNIC, a bank account, proof of income (salary slip or bank statement), and passport-sized photographs. Most brokers now offer online account opening and mobile trading apps.

36. Can overseas Pakistanis invest in PSX?
Yes, overseas Pakistanis can invest in the Pakistan Stock Exchange through the Roshan Digital Account (RDA). RDA was launched by the State Bank of Pakistan and allows non-resident Pakistanis to open bank accounts in Pakistan remotely and invest in government securities and stocks. You can open an RDA through participating banks like HBL, UBL, MCB, and Meezan Bank.

37. What taxes apply on stock trading in Pakistan?
Capital gains tax applies when you sell stocks at a profit. For stocks held less than 1 year, the tax rate is 15%. For stocks held between 1-2 years, the rate is 12.5%. For stocks held more than 2 years, the rate is 0% (tax-free). Additionally, there is a 0.5% withholding tax on the sale value of shares for filers, and 1% for non-filers. Dividend income is taxed at 15% for filers and 30% for non-filers.

38. What is the settlement cycle in Pakistan?
The settlement cycle for stock trades on PSX is T+2, which means the trade is settled 2 business days after the transaction date. So if you buy a stock on Monday, the shares will be delivered to your CDC account and money deducted from your bank account by Wednesday. During this period, the stock is considered "in transit" and you cannot sell it until settlement is complete.

40. What is halal investing in Pakistan?
Halal investing means investing in companies that comply with Islamic principles. This means avoiding companies involved in alcohol, gambling, tobacco, conventional banking (interest-based), or other prohibited activities. On PSX, the KMI-30 (KSE Meezan Index) tracks 30 Shariah-compliant stocks. Meezan Bank and other Islamic brokers offer Shariah-compliant trading accounts. Always check the Shariah screening criteria if halal investing is important to you.

41. What is the difference between shares and stocks?
In everyday language, shares and stocks mean the same thing - they both refer to ownership in a company. Technically, "stock" is a general term for ownership in any company, while "shares" refers to ownership units in a specific company. For example, you might say "I invest in stocks" (general) or "I own 100 shares of OGDC" (specific). On PSX, both terms are used interchangeably.

43. What happens if a company goes bankrupt?
If a company listed on PSX goes bankrupt, its stock price will typically drop to near zero before it is delisted from the exchange. As a shareholder, you may lose most or all of your investment. In liquidation, creditors and bondholders are paid first, and shareholders get whatever is left (which is usually nothing). This is why diversification is so important - if one company fails, your other investments can still protect your wealth.

44. What is insider trading?
Insider trading is when someone buys or sells stocks based on confidential, non-public information about a company. For example, if a company executive knows that the company will announce poor earnings and sells shares before the announcement, that is insider trading. Insider trading is illegal in Pakistan and is punished by SECP with fines and imprisonment. It is considered unfair to other investors who don't have access to the same information.

45. What is a mutual fund?
A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment fund that pools money from many investors and invests it in stocks, bonds, or other securities. Instead of picking individual stocks yourself, you give your money to a fund manager who makes the investment decisions. In Pakistan, mutual funds are offered by companies like Al Meezan, HBL Asset Management, UBL Fund Managers, and JS Investments. Mutual funds are a good option for people who don't have time to research individual stocks.

46. What are the risks of stock market investing?
The main risks of stock market investing are: (1) Market risk - the entire market can fall due to economic downturns, political instability, or global events. (2) Company-specific risk - a single company can fail due to poor management or competition. (3) Liquidity risk - some stocks have low trading volume, making it hard to sell quickly. (4) Currency risk - for foreign investors, PKR depreciation affects returns. (5) Inflation risk - returns may not keep up with inflation. To minimize risk, diversify your investments, invest for the long term, and never invest money you need for daily expenses.

47. How do I sell my stocks?
To sell your stocks: (1) Log in to your broker's trading platform or app. (2) Select the stock you want to sell from your portfolio. (3) Enter the number of shares you want to sell. (4) Choose the order type - market order (sell at current market price) or limit order (sell only at your specified price or higher). (5) Confirm the order. The sale will be executed during trading hours (9:30 AM - 3:30 PM) and settled in T+2 days.

48. What is a limit order vs market order?
A market order is an instruction to buy or sell a stock immediately at the current market price. It guarantees execution but not the exact price. A limit order is an instruction to buy or sell a stock only at a specific price or better. For example, if a stock is trading at Rs. 100, you can place a limit buy order at Rs. 95 - the order will only execute if the price drops to Rs. 95 or below. Limit orders give you price control but may not be executed if the price doesn't reach your limit.

49. What is the right time to invest in stocks?
There is no perfect time to invest. Research shows that trying to time the market (waiting for the "right" moment) usually performs worse than investing regularly over time. A strategy called rupee-cost averaging involves investing a fixed amount every month regardless of market conditions. This way, you buy more shares when prices are low and fewer when prices are high, averaging out your cost over time.

50. How does news affect stock prices?
News can significantly affect stock prices. Positive news (strong earnings, new contracts, favorable government policies) tends to push prices up. Negative news (losses, lawsuits, political instability, global recession) tends to push prices down. Company-specific news affects individual stocks, while economic and political news affects the entire market.
